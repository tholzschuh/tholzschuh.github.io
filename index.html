about me

I’m a master’s student majoring in mathematics at Heidelberg University.

Currently, I’m working on my master’s thesis under Prof. Alexander Schmidt related to a reformulation of Grothendieck’s Section Conjecture in terms of étale homotopy types as a homotopy fixed-point problem.

Before that, I was visiting Kyoto for about a year as a research student at RIMS, where my advisor was Prof. Shinichi Mochizuki.

I obtained my Bachelor’s degree from Heidelberg University at 30/08/2019, where my undergraduate thesis was supervised by Prof. Gebhard Böckle.

mathematical interests

I’m mainly interested in mathematics related to algebraic/arithmetic geometry, although I’ve not yet decided what exact field I want to specialize in.

Currently I am very interested in mathematics surrounding Grothendieck’s Anabelian Conjectures and homotopical methods (especially possible applications of \(\infty\)-category theory) in arithmetic geometry in general.

other interests

Besides mathematics, I also enjoy consuming lots of different kinds of music and literature and to go hiking.

I occasionally play a game of go and sometimes even spend time on developing useless software, mainly written in the functional programming language haskell, like this webpage.
