For this chapter, we fix a category $\mathcal{C}$ satisfying

1.  $\mathcal{C}$ admits *finite limits*

2.  $\mathcal{C}$ admits *finite coproducts* and *quotients by finite
    groups of automorphisms*

3.  Every morphism $X \xrightarrow{u} Y$ of $\mathcal{C}$ factorizes as
    $u = u''\circ u'$ with $u'$ an and $u''$ a and every monomorphism
    $X \xrightarrow{u} Y$ identifies $X$ with a direct summand of $Y$.

For a choice of such a category $\mathcal{C}$ we further fix a functor
$F \colon \mathcal{C} \to \textit{fsets}$ satisfying

1.  $F$ is *left exact*

2.  $F$ *commutes* with coproducts, quotients by finite groups of
    automorphisms and carries epimorphisms into epimorphisms

3.  $F$ is *conservative*, i.e. $u$ is an isomorphism if $Fu$ is

In the following, we will give an alternative characterization of
categories $\mathcal{C}$ satisfying $(G1)$-$(G3)$ and admitting a
functor $F$ satisfying $(G4)$-$(G6)$ resulting in the notion of
$\textit{Galois Category}$
